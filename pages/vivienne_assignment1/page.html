Twin Planets

· Watcher

Communicator ·

These two planets are said to be twins as they share the same 'birth' date and have a lot of the same properties. Both planets are mainly composed of Copper, giving them their reddish appearance. The only differences they show are their sizes and their humanistic features where the smaller planet has a mouth that is always smiling and the larger of the two has an eye that seems to be always watching.

Sleeping Dwarf

This Dwarf planet mimics the motions of breathing, it's whole body expanding in and out where it appears to be in a deep sleep. The eye is firmly shut and Scientists are still unsure as to when it may open.

As this sleeping Dwarf planet 'inhales', the following 'exhale' produces a concentrated amount of Helium through a conical crater found on it's surface.

FACTS

Can you spot the glowing stars?

Believe it or not, beyond our solar system, there is a vortex in space which acts like a well and pulls up bits of H2O into the atmosphere.

Surrounding these warps in space are hundreds of planets in which Scientists believe are highly likely to contain other living creatures where water and oxygen is present

Though there is no strong orbit for these planets to follow and as a result, many planets lose their position near this warp in space or even end up being sucked into the well.

This happens due to the combination gases from 2 seperate areas in which one releases Hydrogen in dust clouds between stars and the other that releases Oxygen.

The combination of heat surrounding this area in space, creates an evaporation of the H2O that is formed, and as a result, clouds are created which are seen surrounding this area.

Star Planet

A glowing planet that can be seen from millions of light years away and is found in an area scattered with brightly glowing stars. Scientists first believed that this planet was a regular star, despite its large size.

However, further studies show a crystallized body, forming around the star suggesting that it retains some level of self-gravity essentially classfiying this star as a core to a planet.
